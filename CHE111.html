INTRODUCTION

Sustainable fashion refers to clothing, accessories, and footwear produced in an environmentally and ethically responsible way. It focuses on minimizing the fashion industry's negative impact on the planet, people, and animals. This includes using eco-friendly materials, reducing waste, promoting fair labor practices, and ensuring transparency throughout the supply chain. Sustainable fashion also encourages durability, timeless designs, and mindful consumption to combat overproduction and fast fashion's environmental harm.

Key aspects of sustainable fashion include:

1. Eco-friendly materials: Using organic, recycled, or plant-based fabrics that have a lower environmental footprint.

2. Ethical production: Ensuring fair wages, safe working conditions, and respect for workers' rights.

3.Waste reduction: Recycling, upcycling, and designing garments for longevity to reduce textile waste.

4. Slow fashion: Focusing on quality, durability, and timeless design rather than following fast-changing trends.

5. Circular economy: Promoting practices like clothing repair, resale, and recycling to extend the lifecycle of garments.

Conclusion

Overall, sustainable fashion is about creating a fashion system that values people, the planet, and long-term viability over short-term profit and disposable consumer culture.
